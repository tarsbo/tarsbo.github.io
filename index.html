Tara Boland • Graphic Designer

ABOUT ME

My name is Tara Boland and I am a graphic designer, studying at Algonquin College. I’m an optimistic, hard-working student with a love for all things design-related. My specialties include illlustration, logo design, and typography.

My whole life, I have been lucky enough to have been surrounded by artistic people. With my mother being in the graphic design industry, I spent a lot of time wandering around studios and quickly fell in love. I’m a graphic designer because I love the entire graphic design process. I love being able to use my creativity to find solutions and create something eyepleasing and memorable. I am a positive thinker, and I think that this sets me apart from other designers as I can see the potential in most anything and believe that there is always a solution.

MY WORK

This is a typography-based”quote book”. The theme throughout the book is The Ocean. I hand made paper coiled letters to create the wavey/watery-looking effects, representing the ocean throughout the spreads.

This is a book cover and full dust jacket re-designed for the novel Fifth Business by Robertson Davies. Having read the novel, I decided to focus on a small piece of symbolism that comes back at the end of the novel — the stone.

This is an event poster designed for a (made up) concert being put on by the Arctic Monkeys. I drew all the images myself and brought them into Illustrator to create the poster.

This is an illustratived, lyrical poster made for the band Foster the People, to the song Best Friend. I hand drew everything seen on the poster, including the type, and then brought it into Illustrator to add colours. I chose the colours based on the colours used in the Supermodel album cover.

This is an illustration of Alice Cooper, rock-and-roll legend, created in a style inspired by that of artist, Pablo Lobato. The goal in this piece was to create a recognizable celebrity using simple 2-D shapes, as well as other specific techniques. I used Adobe Illustrator to create this piece.

This is a campaign design for Tiffany & Co. This campaign focuses on the theme of engagement, and most prominently, their The Tiffany engagement ring. I first drew out each character, then brought the drawings into Illustrator where I further editted them.

This is a Rolling Stone Magazine spread mock-up about rapper, Nicki Minaj. I wanted the spread to reflect Nicki Minaj’s persona and being, and therefore added pops of pink across the spread. I used the grid in many different ways to create a unique feel. Nicki Minaj is an independent woman who stands out and I reflected this in my spread by keeping photos to a minimum, with one large image creating a high-impact focal point.
